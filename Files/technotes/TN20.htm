Manufacturers

The biggest manufacturer of Symbian OS devices is Nokia. They are also, by far, the largest manufacturer of phones in the world. While they hold a small share of the North American marketplace, worldwide, they are the dominant manufacturer. This is the opposite of Palm, which has always been strongest in the US.

Other manufacturers of Symbian OS phones include Sony Ericsson, Samsung, Motorola and LG.

Devices

A much wider variety of devices is available than from Palm. Every possible form factor is manufactured, including touch and non-touch, candy bar, flip phone, slider phone, various keyboard layouts, with different screen sizes and resolutions, with cameras and without, etc.

While this creates opportunity, it also can be a challenge. Programs set up for one device may not run the exact same way on another. NS Basic/Symbian OS has a number of features to help deal with this.

The Operating System

Symbian OS is an operating system designed for use in mobile phones. While a few of its features are somewhat Windows like (there is a C drive, for example) it is a completely different operating system. It makes good use of the limited resources a smartphone has and has much better security. The user interface is separate from the operating system itself: the most commonly used ones for smartphones are S60 and UIQ.

Versions of the OS

The S60 Platform is the most commonly used user interface on SmartPhone devices. The latest version of it is 3rd edition: NS Basic/Symbian OS supports S60 Third Edition phones. The next edition will be the 5th Edition: no 4th Edition is expected. S60 is used on Nokia devices. Most S60 devices do not have touchscreens, but touch screen devices are expected in late 2008.

The UIQ Platform is the next most commonly used platform. The latest version is 3.3. NS Basic/Symbian OS supports UIQ 3.x devices. UIQ devices are manufactured by Sony Ericsson and Motorola. Most UIQ devices have touchscreens.

It has been announced that the Symbian OS will become open source. S60 and UIQ will be absorbed into the Symbian OS group and a single version, based on S60, will eventually appear.
